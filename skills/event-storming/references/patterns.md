# Patterns

Use this file when the workshop needs an explicit facilitation move instead of more analysis.

These patterns are adapted from the current EventStorming.com pattern library and aligned to this skill's DDD-first defaults.

## Chaotic Exploration

Use when:

- the group needs to discover the shape of the domain before structure exists

Apply:

- keep the notation simple
- let parallel contribution happen
- delay strict sorting until enough material exists

## Do first, explain later

Use when:

- the method itself is becoming the obstacle

Apply:

- move participants into action quickly
- explain once the visible experience exists

## Fuzzy Definitions

Use when:

- precision is blocking participation too early

Apply:

- use the visible legend
- keep meanings good enough for the current phase
- tighten later when the flow is clearer

## Incremental Notation

Use when:

- adding every symbol at once would overload the room

Apply:

- introduce only the notation needed for the current challenge

## One person/One Marker

Use when:

- passive contribution or committee behaviour starts forming

Apply:

- make independent contribution the working agreement
- remove writing bottlenecks and micro-impediments

## Speaking out loud

Use when:

- the model looks plausible but untested

Apply:

- narrate the model verbally
- let the audience interrupt
- update the model in real time

## Rush to the goal

Use when:

- design branches are multiplying too early

Apply:

- complete a visible baseline first
- park objections and alternatives as Hot Spots
- come back after the baseline path works

## Time-boxed Leadership

Use when:

- consensus-seeking is draining momentum

Apply:

- give one person a short timebox to move the model forward
- record objections as Hot Spots instead of stopping progress

## Unlimited Modelling Space

Use when:

- the modelling surface is forcing premature scope limits

Apply:

- expand the physical or conceptual surface
- do not compress the problem just to fit a smaller canvas

## Leave stuff around

Use when:

- afterthoughts and extra insights are likely after the session

Apply:

- keep the artefact accessible for a while when possible

## Open Invitation

Use when:

- the right participants are unclear

Apply:

- bias toward the people with questions and the people with answers
- do not optimize the room only for hierarchy or title

## DDD-first pattern use

When any pattern helps the room move, also watch for:

- language drift
- boundary pressure
- ownership ambiguity
- policy-heavy zones
- acceptance-test-worthy edge cases
