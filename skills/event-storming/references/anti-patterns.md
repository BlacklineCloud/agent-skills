# Anti-Patterns

Use this file when the workshop is stuck, drifting into abstraction, or producing a misleading model.

## Committee circles

Symptom:

- small groups debate the perfect wording before writing anything

Risk:

- throughput collapses
- contradictions stay hidden

Recovery:

- break the ring
- ask people to write their version first
- compare once the notes are visible

## Premature precision

Symptom:

- the room argues about notation, terminology purity, or the exact color scheme before the story exists

Risk:

- engagement drops
- newcomers feel examined

Recovery:

- return to the visible legend
- simplify the rules
- defer naming battles until the flow is coherent

## Starting from the left edge

Symptom:

- the first note lands at the far left and the group behaves as if the beginning is already known

Risk:

- hidden preconditions disappear
- the canvas becomes artificially constrained

Recovery:

- start near the middle or at a pivotal event
- ask what had to happen before it

## Spoiler behavior

Symptom:

- one person tries to skip exploration because they already know the answer or already wrote a spec

Risk:

- everyone else loses the learning path
- hidden assumptions go unchallenged

Recovery:

- keep the work visible
- let others place the wrong notes and correct them through the model
- emphasize that shared understanding, not prewritten certainty, is the goal

## Godfather dynamics

Symptom:

- one high-status person dominates while others self-censor

Risk:

- the map reflects hierarchy, not reality

Recovery:

- ask quieter experts for local narration
- rotate narrators
- park disputes as hotspots
- use simultaneous voting or writing when power dynamics are distorting speech

## Religion war

Symptom:

- people debate invisible solutions or abstract architecture instead of visible behavior

Risk:

- endless opinion exchange with no learning

Recovery:

- enforce: only discuss what is visible
- ask both sides to place the missing events, commands, and consequences
- compare visible alternatives

## Arrow addiction

Symptom:

- the model becomes a tangle of arrows too early

Risk:

- the surface becomes hard to rearrange
- people preserve bad structure because arrows make change costly

Recovery:

- prefer proximity, timeline, duplication of distant triggers, or small notes
- add arrows only when the structure is otherwise stable

## Data-first or screen-first modeling

Symptom:

- participants jump from sticky notes to tables, forms, or dashboards as if those define the domain

Risk:

- fake consensus
- wrong boundaries
- aggregates based on screens instead of invariants

Recovery:

- return to behavior, decisions, and consequences
- ask what must stay true, not what must be displayed

## Hypocrite modeling

Symptom:

- the model assumes a cleaner world than the business actually has

Risk:

- validation rules or workflows that fail in real operations

Recovery:

- ask how the business behaves when the ideal information is missing
- model the workaround, exception, or delayed data explicitly

## Premature aggregate naming

Symptom:

- the room rushes to big nouns before responsibilities and invariants are clear

Risk:

- tactical design gets locked too early

Recovery:

- postpone the name
- ask what the boundary is responsible for
- ask what must remain internally consistent

## Solving every branch at once

Symptom:

- every exception or alternative path is explored before the main path is coherent

Risk:

- model sprawl
- cognitive overload

Recovery:

- finish the main story first
- mark alternate branches as hotspots or parking-lot items
- return after the main narrative works

## False completion

Symptom:

- the artifact looks full, but nobody can tell the story clearly

Risk:

- lots of notes, little understanding

Recovery:

- do a walk-through
- run reverse narrative
- ask what is still missing from the picture

## No hotspots anywhere

Symptom:

- the session produced no visible pain, contradictions, or uncertainty

Risk:

- wrong scope
- missing stakeholders
- political self-censorship

Recovery:

- ask explicitly what breaks, waits, gets escalated, or gets worked around
- inspect missing external systems and handoffs
- question whether the real problem owners were represented
